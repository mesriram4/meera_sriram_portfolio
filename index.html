About

Who I am

I graduated with a double major in Cognitive Sciences and Music — Integrated Composition, Improvisation, and Technology at UC Irvine. My academic path has been shaped by one unifying obsession: the relationship between pattern, perception, and meaning — whether found in neural signals, datasets, or sound.

In the Cognitive Neural Computation Lab, I spent over a year working with Dr. Megan Peters on questions at the frontier of consciousness research — specifically whether first-order neural correlates can explain perceptual inflation. I collected fMRI data, built MATLAB analysis pipelines, and co-authored a paper accepted to CCN 2026.

My music work is inseparable from data. My senior project, Muse of the Cosmos, translated astronomical data into musical composition through sonification. I also premiered Correlations at the 10th SIDIMPRO international festival in Ensenada, Mexico — a piece built entirely from scatter plots used as a graphic score.

I'm looking for roles where these worlds overlap: data analytics, research operations, music technology, or any space that rewards both rigor and creativity.
